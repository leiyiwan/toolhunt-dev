**VistaCreate vs Easil: Compare design tools for marketers in 2026. Brand kits, templates, and pricing.** Both platforms promise faster, cheaper graphic design than Canva or Adobe Express, but they target different marketing workflows. VistaCreate leans into stock asset libraries and drag-and-drop simplicity, while Easil focuses on brand consistency and team collaboration. Here’s how they stack up head-to-head — no fluff, just the facts.

## Quick Verdict

VistaCreate wins for solo marketers and small businesses that need a massive library of templates and stock elements without a subscription. Easil is better for agencies and marketing teams that require strict brand governance, multi-user brand kits, and collaborative approval workflows. If you already use VistaPrint for print products, VistaCreate integrates cleanly. If brand compliance is your top headache, Easil’s brand kit controls are unmatched.

## Features Deep Dive

### Template Libraries & Customization

VistaCreate claims over 100,000 templates across social media, print, video, and web. Many are what you’d expect: polished, trend-aware, and easy to remix. The real advantage is the stock library — 75 million photos and videos from Depositphotos, accessible directly in the editor. You can swap any placeholder image with a licensed stock shot in one click. On the downside, too many templates feel similar; the sheer volume can be overwhelming.

Easil takes a different approach. It offers roughly 8,000 templates, but each is designed with brand consistency in mind. Every template comes with pre-built “Brand Blocks” — elements you can lock or restrict so users can’t change colors, fonts, or logos. For a marketing team managing 20+ accounts, this is a lifesaver. The template quality is high, but the catalog is smaller; you’ll occasionally struggle to find a specific niche design.

### Brand Kits: The Deciding Factor

This is where Easil pulls ahead. On the Plus plan ($7.50/month), you get unlimited brand kits. Each kit can include multiple brand color palettes, font pairs, logo placements, and even custom brand rules (like “always use left-aligned headline”). You can lock any element in a template — text, images, backgrounds — so team members only edit the parts you allow. VistaCreate’s brand kit is simpler: one kit on Pro, limited to colors, fonts, and logo upload. No element locking, no brand rules. If you’re a single user, that’s fine. If you’re managing a junior designer or a client’s intern, you’ll want Easil.

### Collaboration & Workflow

VistaCreate’s team plan ($28/month for up to 5 users) adds shared folders and basic commenting. It works, but it’s basic — think Google Docs-level feedback. There’s no version history (only the last save is kept) and no approval workflow. For a two-person startup, that’s enough. For an agency with multiple rounds of client revisions, it’s not.

Easil’s Edge plan ($22/month for up to 5 users) includes version history, approval requests, and role-based permissions. You can assign roles like “Editor” (can change anything), “Brand Contributor” (can only change unlocked elements), and “Viewer” (read-only). Approvals can be sent to external clients without requiring them to create an account. This workflow cuts down on the “I changed the font again” chaos.

### Animation & Video

Both tools offer basic animation. VistaCreate has a timeline-based video editor and supports GIFs, MP4 exports, and animated text effects. It’s fine for social media posts that need a little motion. Easil’s animation is more limited — animated text, sliding elements, fade-ins — but works reliably. For full video editing, neither beats Canva or Adobe Express; they’re adequate for simple social videos.

## User Experience & Ease of Use

VistaCreate’s interface is straightforward. The left panel holds elements, uploads, text, and backgrounds. Drag-and-drop is responsive. The search bar is decent but can return irrelevant results because of the huge stock library. Beginners will feel at home quickly. The mobile app is functional for light edits — resizing, text changes — but not for building from scratch.

Easil has a steeper learning curve, mainly because of the brand kit and locking system. Once you set up a brand kit, everyday editing becomes faster. The editor is clean, with a top toolbar and side panels. The drag-and-drop is smooth, but resizing objects sometimes feels less precise than in VistaCreate. The mobile app is iOS-only and limited; you can’t create a new design from scratch on a phone. That’s a gap if your team works on tablets often.

## Pricing & Value

**VistaCreate Free:** You get 5,000+ templates, 50,000+ stock photos (with Depositphotos watermark on some), and limited exports (JPG, PNG, PDF up to 3000px). Good for testing, but watermarks on premium assets get annoying fast.

**VistaCreate Pro ($10/month annual):** Unlocks all templates, 75M+ stock assets without watermarks, exports up to 8000px, background removal, and social scheduling. For one user, this is a steal — cheaper than Canva Pro ($13/month) and includes more stock images.

**VistaCreate Team ($28/month):** Up to 5 users. Adds shared folders and commenting. No per-user pricing, so it’s expensive if you only need 2 seats but cheap if you max it out.

**Easil Free:** 5GB storage, 1 brand kit (limited), watermarked exports. Viable for testing but not for production.

**Easil Plus ($7.50/month annual):** Unlimited brand kits, 100GB storage, no watermarks, up to 4000px exports. Best value if you need multiple brand kits.

**Easil Edge ($22/month annual):** Up to 5 users, version history, approvals, role-based permissions, 500GB storage. The collaboration features justify the price for teams.

VistaCreate is cheaper for a single user with stock needs; Easil is cheaper for a team needing brand controls (5 users on Edge vs VistaCreate Team saves $6/month).

## Pros & Cons

### VistaCreate

**Pros**
- Massive stock library integrated into editor.
- Lower entry price for Pro than Canva or Easil Edge.
- Print-ready exports with CMYK and bleed.
- Social media scheduling built-in (Pro).
- Good for solo creators who never touch brand guidelines.

**Cons**
- Brand kit limited to one kit on Pro (upgrade to Team for more? No, Team still one kit).
- No element locking or brand rule enforcement.
- Version history missing; team collaboration is barebones.
- Template library suffers from “quantity over quality” in some categories.

### Easil

**Pros**
- Best-in-class brand kit controls (lock elements, brand rules, unlimited kits).
- Approval workflow and version history for teams.
- Role-based permissions (Editor, Brand Contributor, Viewer).
- External client review without account creation.

**Cons**
- Smaller template library; fewer niche designs.
- Mobile app is iOS-only and edit-limited (no creation from scratch).
- No built-in social scheduling (requires third-party).
- Steeper initial learning curve.

## Final Recommendation

**Choose VistaCreate if** you’re a solo marketer, a small business owner, or someone who frequently prints materials. The stock library and low price make it ideal for churning out high-volume social graphics and flyers without worrying about brand compliance.

**Choose Easil if** you run an agency, manage multiple brands, or have a team that needs to stay on-brand without constant supervision. The brand kit features save hours of manual review. It’s also the better pick if you work with external clients who need to approve designs.

If you’re torn, consider a one-month trial of both. Start with VistaCreate Pro ($10) — if you find yourself fighting with brand consistency, switch to Easil Plus ($7.50). Your wallet and your sanity will thank you.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use VistaCreate and Easil for free forever?**  
A: Yes, both have free plans. VistaCreate free includes watermarks on some stock assets; Easil free limits exports to watermarked files and 5GB storage. Both are usable for personal projects but not professional outputs.

**Q: Which tool has better print templates?**  
A: VistaCreate — it offers more print-specific templates (business cards, flyers, menus) with CMYK exports and bleed settings. Easil supports CMYK but its print template selection is smaller.

**Q: Do either integrate with Canva or Adobe Express?**  
A: No. They are direct competitors. You can import SVG or PNG files from other tools, but there’s no native integration.

**Q: Can I schedule social media posts directly from Easil?**  
A: No. Easil doesn’t offer built-in scheduling. You’ll need to export and upload to a scheduler like Buffer or Hootsuite. VistaCreate Pro has direct scheduling to Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, and LinkedIn.

**Q: Which tool handles team approvals better?**  
A: Easil. Its approval workflow lets you send designs for review, track version history, and get sign-offs. VistaCreate’s commenting is too basic for structured approvals.

**Q: Is there a mobile app for Easil on Android?**  
A: No. Easil’s mobile app is iOS-only. VistaCreate offers both iOS and Android apps, though editing features are limited on both.
